What is a Triage?

According to the Merriam Webster Dictionary, a triage is "the sorting of patients (as in an emergency room) according to the urgency of their need for care."

By knowing how to sort the order of patients by severity of injury, that allows a institution to prioritize the highest risk patients first. This allows the highest priority patients to get the urgent care they need.

How is this helpful?

Who is this for?

Institutions like hospitals, urgent care facilities, veterinarian clinics, and others would be able to sort their incoming patients, streamlining the process of patient intake and allowing for a more efficient workplace.
